# 0011 — NH35 bridge study: terraced relief + design-the-cover-last

Jon supplied the isolated NH35 decorative bridge. Two adopted lessons:

1. **The wave should be TERRACED, not outlined.** The NH35's waves are
   stepped crescent plateaus in z — light catches each step edge. Rev B
   swells become three relief terraces (~1mm steps) cascading toward the
   balance well; the crest is the tallest terrace curling at the rim;
   the well may open into a C like the NH35's. Sunburst/Geneva graining
   flows across terraces in the finishing pass. Prints and mills cleanly.

2. **Sequence: pack the movement first, sculpt the cover last.** The
   bridge's mechanical interfaces are frozen and tested (5 bearings,
   well, feet). Its OUTLINE and terracing wait until everything under
   it exists: balance cock, escapement, keyless works, dial-side moon.
   Revised order: 2c balance cock + escapement -> 2d keyless works ->
   2e motion works + moon (dial side) -> 2f THE BRIDGE beauty pass with
   the full movement packed -> Jon's gate on the complete composition.
